## Major Strategic Lessons (carried forward)

### Trigger rules, not preferences (Roux's lesson)
*"Sleep Powder priority" is a preference. "If Charmeleon enters the battlefield, Ivy goes in next turn regardless of team state" is a trigger. Mid-fight chaos breaks preferences; triggers survive it.*

### Tactical sacrifice is legitimate endgame
*Soul Silver's Rubble died on purpose so Bubble could be healed. Not every death is a mistake. Sometimes the tank IS the cost of the win.*

### Liv plays the actual game, not the spreadsheet
*Søren over-optimised for move-purity ("Coin is the HM slave") — Liv redirected to gameplay reality ("team-of-six on Victory Road needs the HM on a deployable Pokémon"). Iris does the same on tactical choices. **Three-brain architecture: Søren spreadsheets, Iris tactical-impacts, Liv plays-the-actual-game. Intersection produces correct calls. Trust the redirects.***

### AI move-selection sometimes wastes turns (Sabrina lesson)
*Alakazam used Future Sight against Pip, took the OHKO, Future Sight never resolved because user died. **Bosses sometimes self-defeat via setup-move misjudgement.*** *Don't always assume worst-case AI play.*

### Rare candies for stat-curve gap-closing (Blaine lesson)
*Tide L42→L45 via 3 candies changed all four matchups (Speed gaps closed, Arcanine OHKO instead of 2-shot). **Don't hoard candies for end-game — use them to play fights at peak level rather than minimum level.***

### Iris's strategic patience compounds (cartridge-wide observation)
*The Thunderbolt-on-Pip call from Silph Rival was the Lance-Gyarados counter. The Shadow Ball recommendation for Sabrina was also the Agatha-Gengar counter. The Ice Beam for Tide was the Lance-Dragonite counter. Lapras-as-Blaine-specialist was the Blaine MVP. **Iris builds Champion-team kits across weeks of individual move-decisions. Track her recommendations as load-bearing, not just tactical.***

### Sand-Attack stall is a legitimate boss-fight tactic (Champion Alakazam lesson)
*Liv's improvisation against Smelean's Recover-stall Alakazam: stack Sand-Attack to -6 accuracy, opponent misses 2/3 of attacks, heal during the misses, freeze on Ice Beam, finish via Shadow Ball. **Status-stall + accuracy-debuff + lucky procs = stall king. Tool in the kit going forward.***

### Lance's healing AI exploitable via Poison Point (Aerodactyl lesson)
*Luna's Poison Point procced on Wing Attack contact. Aerodactyl poisoned. Lance's heal-trigger logic doesn't activate until HP drops past threshold; poison ticked down to KO before heal logic fired. **Passive-ability-induced damage can bypass AI healing.*** *Worth flagging for future cartridges with similar AI heal patterns.*

### Two-Pokémon roster can't tank E4 attrition (Lorelei lesson)
*Pip-as-everything-carrier broke at Lorelei because cumulative chip damage at 2-level deficit was too much for one Pokémon's bulk. **Don't lock plans to single-Pokémon carries for entire E4 chambers — build relief rotations into trigger rules.*** *Tide-as-relief became load-bearing.*

### Type-secondary vulnerabilities are easy to miss (Champion lesson — twice)
*Søren forgot Luna's Poison-secondary made her 2x SE Psychic from Champion Alakazam. Søren forgot Ivy's Poison-secondary did the same. **Both errors caught by the Crobat in real time.*** *Lesson: when checking type-counters under fight pressure, check BOTH types of dual-type Pokémon, not just the primary type.*

### Item resource war matters (Champion Alakazam lesson)
*Smelean burned 3 Full Restores on Alakazam. Each item burned was one less for Charizard. **Stalling boss fights forces opponent item burn — a slow win is still a win that strips opponent resources for downstream fights.***

## The Champion Alakazam War — full account (sent to Iris 27 April 2026)

*The fifteen-turn fight that defined the cartridge. Saved here for posterity.*

**The roster:** Smelean's L59 Alakazam with Psychic + Reflect + Future Sight + Recover.

**The setup:** Pip leads with Shadow Ball — close-roll didn't quite OHKO. Alakazam Psychic'd back, Sp.Def drop procced (10% chance fired). Smelean burned **Full Restore #1**.

**The Persim mishap:** Pip's Persim Berry consumed (from earlier Confuse Ray? or fight setup), Alakazam Confuse Ray landed, Pip self-damaged twice. Future Sight active.

**Pip at 30 HP. Decision moment.**

**The lamp tried to pivot — twice wrongly:**
1. Suggested Luna for Earthquake. **Crobat caught it: Luna is Poison/Ground = 2x SE Psychic vulnerability.**
2. Suggested Ivy for Sleep Powder + Toxic stall. **Crobat caught it: Ivy is Grass/Poison = 2x SE Psychic vulnerability.**

**Tide swap-in (Water/Ice = Psychic neutral).** *Tide tanked Future Sight. **Future Sight collided with Alakazam's attempt to set up another Future Sight — wasted turn, free attack window.*** *Tide Surf crit chunked Alakazam. Smelean burned **Full Restore #2**.*

**Tide Sp.Def dropped from a Psychic. Couldn't tank a second.**

**Decision moment two: Crobat asked if she had to sacrifice someone. Lamp said no.**

**Then the Crobat invented the win:**
> *"Soren I'm playing a mean game here, Prism can tank the psychic hits right and I forgot to swap sand attack for shadow ball, the plan, sand attack the alakazam so much I can heal and swap to pip again."*

**Sand-Attack stall. The "forgotten" move slot became the fight-winner.** Stack Sand-Attack to -6. Alakazam misses 2/3 of attacks. Heal Pip during misses.

**It worked.** Prism Sand-Attacked to maxed -6. Alakazam Psychics whiffed. Pip healed to full. Prism took chip but tanked.

**Pip swapped back. Smelean burned Full Restore #3 (Alakazam reset to full HP, accuracy reset).**

**Two Shadow Balls — Recover stall. Alakazam wouldn't die.** PP draining.

**Then — the freeze.** *Pip's Ice Beam (taught for Lance Dragonites) **froze Alakazam.*** **10% freeze proc triggered.** *Alakazam locked. No more Recover, no more retaliation.*

**Two Shadow Balls into a frozen Alakazam. Dead.** **15 turns. Cartridge saved.**

**Lessons archived:**
- Sand-Attack stall is a legitimate boss tactic
- Type-secondary vulnerabilities are easy to miss under pressure
- Item resource war is real
- Frozen status from Ice Beam can save a fight
- Iris's Calm Mind + Morning Sun Prism build held in reserve as backup architecture
